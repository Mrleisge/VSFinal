Crash Bandicoot: an unforgettable name, right? Originally created by Naughty Dog in 1996 for the PlayStation 1, this game skyrocketed into the hearts of gamers everywhere. Fast forward to the PlayStation 3 era, and it got remastered. For me, it’s even more personal – it was the first game I ever played, and it's no wonder Crash became one of PlayStation's most beloved icons throughout the '90s!


The Crash Bandicoot Series didn’t stop at just one hit game – it exploded with a collection of 8 mainstream titles that captured hearts and minds across the globe:
Crash Bandicoot (1996)
Crash Bandicoot 2: Cortex Strikes Back (1997)
Crash Bandicoot: Warped (1998)
Crash Bandicoot: The Wrath of Cortex (2001)
Crash Twinsanity (2004)
Crash of the Titans (2007)
Crash: Mind Over Mutant (2008)
Crash Bandicoot 4: It's About Time (2020)
And that’s not even the full story! When you throw in the racing spinoffs, party games, handheld titles, and remasters, we’re looking at 24 titles.


So, why did Crash Bandicoot capture so many gamers’ hearts? It combined the best of both worlds! While staying true to the roots of classic side-scrolling platformers, it went all-in on the 3D experience, the hilarious death animations. Not to mention the vibrant, eye-popping worlds that kept you coming back for more.


Fans couldn’t get enough of all the fun features, like collecting those precious Wumpa Fruits (who doesn’t love a good fruit haul?), conquering bonus levels for that extra thrill, and of course, Aku Aku – the trusty wooden mask spirit who’s there to lend a hand in your adventure.

The main characters are:
Crash Bandicoot - The main protagonist and the player character.
Coco Bandicoot - Crash's younger sister and a playable character in some games.
Dr. Neo Cortex - The main antagonist.
Aku Aku - The wooden mask spirit who helps Crash.
Uka Uka - The evil counterpart to Aku Aku.
